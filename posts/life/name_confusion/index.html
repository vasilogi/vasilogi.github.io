Name Di-confusion

This is my first post and instead of a proper introduction, I would like to clarify my name’s situation. I intend do that bacause many non-Greek friends and colleagues get confused since my name is different on official documents than how I normally write it.

The situation

Formal and informal

I’m baptised in a Greek Orthodox Christian church (not my choice 😁) as Ιωάννης which directly translates to English as Ioannis. However, this name in Greek sounds too formal and thus its diminutive form, Γιάννης, is preferred.

Phonetic mismatch in alphabets 😯

My experience says that if you are not a Greek, Spanish, French or Arabic speaker then you really get funny when trying to pronounce the initial letter of my name in its informal version. And that is the letter Γ, γ. The actual sound is like the sound made by a dog just before starting really barging but still in angry mode and before adding the rrrr in ggggrrrrr. Additionally, I do not know any 1-1 correspondance of Γ, γ to an English letter. Here comes my confusion. Naturally, we translate Γ, γ to G, g and that’s why often, I write my name as Giannis. Unfortunately, when I moved abroad, people were calling me of course “Giannis” which changes the sound of the original one. After long internal fights, I decided to call my self “Yannis” so as others can mimic the Γ sound.

Vocative case

In Greek, we’ve got five grammatical cases for nouns. Vocative is among them. It’s a grammatical case which is used for a noun that identifies a person being addressed. Specifically, all male names in Greek end with ς (= s), but when you address someone you simply cut-off the ending. Thus, you don’t say “Hey, Yannis” but rather “Hey, Yanni”. But of course, the majority of English speakers don’t follow that, so my non-Greek friends get confused when my Greek friends are calling me Yanni.

Etymology

My name is one the most common names in the world and of course it’s one of the most common male names in Greek. There are two versions regarding the origin of the name: i. the name Ιωάννης comes from the old Jewish name יהוחנן Yəhôḥānān which means «Grace of the God», ii. or from the mythological Greek name Ίωνας (son of Apollo and king and founder of Ionians, one of the four ancient Greek tribes). Meditteranean is an ancient hot pot and wondering about origins is not always the best thing to do. Anyway, here is the version of my name in some modern languages:

I will better not go deeper with my family name. Btw, my name day is on January 7, and you can call me John 😉.
